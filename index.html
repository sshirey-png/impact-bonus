Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about the Impact Bonus

G General Questions

Yes! If you meet your individual performance targets AND your school achieves a 3+ point SPS increase, you can earn both bonuses. For example, a K-8 content teacher could earn up to $3,750 ($3,000 Exceptional + $750 Schoolwide).

As long as your leave is paid leave (using your accrued PTO, sick time, or short-term disability), you remain eligible. Only unpaid leave disqualifies you from the bonus.

Teachers who transfer mid-year will have their performance evaluated based on students they taught for the majority of the school year. Contact your principal or HR for specific guidance on your situation.

A Assessment Questions

A band jump is when a student moves up one or more performance levels from their prior year's LEAP score. For example, moving from "Approaching Basic" to "Basic" or from "Basic" to "Mastery" counts as a band jump.

Conditional growth measures whether students met their individual growth targets based on their starting point. A student at the 50th percentile for conditional growth has met their expected growth target given where they started.

The DIBELS composite combines scores from multiple subtests (letter naming fluency, phoneme segmentation, nonsense word fluency, oral reading fluency) into one overall score. Students are categorized as "at/above benchmark," "below benchmark," or "well below benchmark."

S SPED Teacher Questions

Inclusion/Resource: Teachers who support students with mild to moderate disabilities in general education settings or pull-out resource rooms. Students take standard DIBELS, MAP, and LEAP assessments.

Discovery: Teachers who serve students with significant cognitive disabilities. Students take alternate assessments (DIBELS Alt, Unique Learning System, LEAP Connect).

Your bonus targets are based on the students on your caseload who you provide direct instruction to in ELA and/or Math. Students must be enrolled for the majority of the year and have valid pre/post assessment data.

M Multiple Subjects / Split Assignments

If you teach multiple tested subjects (ELA and Math), your performance targets require meeting goals in BOTH subjects to qualify. Your bonus amount is based on whichever tier you achieve across all your subjects.

Your targets are determined by your primary teaching assignment. If you spend the majority of your time teaching a tested subject, those targets apply. Consult with your principal if your assignment is split evenly.

E Enrichment Teacher Questions

Enrichment teachers have different bonus amounts ($1,500 Exceptional / $750 Full) because their targets are based on programmatic outcomes rather than state standardized test scores. These targets focus on student engagement, skill development, and program quality.

Participation is calculated as the percentage of enrolled students who actively participate in the concert or performance. An 80% participation rate means at least 80% of your students perform. Documentation of attendance/participation is required.
